# Scalar Triple Product

The scalar triple product involves three vectors $\vec{a}$, $\vec{b}$, and $\vec{c}$. It combines the cross product and the dot product to yield a scalar.

## Formula
$$[\vec{a}, \vec{b}, \vec{c}] = (\vec{a} \times \vec{b}) \cdot \vec{c}$$

## Properties
| Property | Formula / Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cyclic Order** | $(\vec{a} \times \vec{b}) \cdot \vec{c} = (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) \cdot \vec{a} = (\vec{c} \times \vec{a}) \cdot \vec{b}$ |
| **Sign Change** | $[\vec{a}, \vec{b}, \vec{c}] = -[\vec{c}, \vec{b}, \vec{a}]$ (reversing any two vectors) |

## Geometric Meaning: Volume
The absolute value of the scalar triple product represents the **volume of the parallelepiped** spanned by the three vectors.

- **Base Area:** $\|\vec{a} \times \vec{b}\|$
- **Height:** $\|\vec{c}\| \cos(\phi)$, where $\phi$ is the angle between $\vec{c}$ and the normal to the base ($\vec{a} \times \vec{b}$).
- **Volume:** $|(\vec{a} \times \vec{b}) \cdot \vec{c}|$
